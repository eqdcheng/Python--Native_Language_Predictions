  • I don't have friends to practice the English with, and I never went to an English school. Because of this, my "listening" is pretty poor.

  • I think that i can understand a sixty-- even a seventy-- percent of what I read, (the dashes I inserted are called "em dashes". Normally, an em dash is just a long-ish line, but I don't know how to insert a real em dash online)

 
  • but without a teacher nor a school i think i'll can´t grow up my poor knowledge

  • but without a teacher nor or a school, I don't think i'll can´t I can grow up improve my poor knowledge. (If you would like to use the word "nor", begin the phrase with "neither". "...but with neither a teacher nor a school..." Also, "nor" is becoming a little archaic and, for the most part, can only be found in literature and scholarly writing).

I'm not sure if this is technically right, but it doesn't quite sound right. You could also say "I'd appreciate it if you could correct me".)
