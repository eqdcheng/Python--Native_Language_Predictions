  • introduce

  • Introduction (Right idea, "Introduce" is the verb, "Introduction" is the Noun)

 
  • Hello,it's the first time I use lang-8.

  • Hello, it's the first time I have used lang-8. (Even though you're still using lang-8 for the first time this action is put into past tense.)

 
  • I' m a senior 3 student,

  • I' m a senior student, in my 3rd year. (Senior student and 3rd year of study are two separate expressions.)

 
  • and one year later I'll face the College Entrance Examination.

  • and in one year (alternatively: in one year's time) I'll face the College Entrance Examination. (Using"one year later" sounds like it's already happened like in a story. Using "in one year/in one year's time" it sounds like you haven't done it yet.)

 
  • It's cruel for us to have this test.

  • It's cruel for us to have this test (suggestion: so soon). (This is a very strong statement. Cruel is such a strong word usually implying abuse. Using "so soon" at the end of the sentence gives a reason for you thinking that it's cruel.)

 
  • I'm good at science and mathmatics.

  • I'm (suggestion: quite) good at science and mathmatics. (This sentence is fine but saying that you are good at something can sometimes be interpreted as immodest. Using "quite" softens the sentence's tone.)

 
  • But my English is not well.

  • But my English is not very good not well. (Alternatively: But I'm not very good at English) (:-D. "not well" is the expression used for a person who feels sick or a situation that is not going well eg A: How are you? B: I'm not well. OR A: How's the party going? B: Not well. It's not used for ability. It made me laugh though :-))

 
  • Sometimes I can get high scores but I know that I just know how to get high scores and I can't use English in communication.

  • Sometimes I can get high scores but I know that I just know how to get high scores and I can't use English in communication. (I understand this sentence but the phrasing and using "know" twice makes it confusing. Consider paraphrasing like so: "Sometimes I get high scores in tests but I can't actually communicate in English I only know how to get high scores at school." This way we've cut out the second know and separated the two phrases containing "get high scores". This is less confusing for the reader.)

 
  • Every sentence I speak must be considered to be exactly right,I will pay attention to its tense and form.

  • (Suggestion: At University) every sentence I speak must be considered to be exactly right (Alternative: considered completely correct) , I have to will pay attention to it's tense and form. (Because you have just finished writing about communicating in English and using English at school you should let the reader know which one you're writing about in this sentence. Using "exactly right" would be understood but "completely correct" is the grammatically correct way to write this. "Will" is future tense "have to" is present. )

 
  • It is a waste of time and my spoken English is extremly terrible.

  • My English Course (Alternatively: The course I am doing) is a waste of time, and my spoken English is extremly terrible. (Again, it is nice to give context to your reader when it is not clear what you're talking about. Using "and" implied that the 2 clauses are 2 separate "terrible" things. Without "and" you imply that the reason why the course is a waste of time is because you don't think you can speak English well.)

 
  • I don't like it, I want to study applied English,not examinatorial English.

  • (Suggestion: I don't like it) (Suggestion: I'd like I want) to study applied English, not examinatorial English. (I use suggestions because your sentence is correct however the words suggest a very high level of hate here. Also "I don't like it, I want to study applied English, not examinatorial English" sounds quite blunt and confrontational for the reader.)

 
  • If you want to know more about Chinese people and you want to help me,please add me to your friends list.

  • If you want to know more about Chinese people and you want to help me, please add me to your friends list. (Perfectly formed!!)

 

I hope that you can not only understand what I'm saying but I hope you find it helpful.
