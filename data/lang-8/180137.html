I spend the time working or researching things on my computer, but here is the problem :

I don't have a good chair to sit on, mine is just wood and some king of straws...

  • Today, my back hurts pretty bad...

  • Today, my back hurts pretty badly...(<--This is the correct way to say this, but you will hear people say something hurts "bad" all the time because it is slang. So, since this is a casual journal entry, I don't think anyone would mind it. :D )

 
  • I spend the time working or researching things on my computer, but here is the problem :

  • I spend the lots of time working or researching things on my computer, but here is the problem: (<--The word "the" is very specific, so if you place it in front of something, it puts emphasis on that thing as being a particular object (perhaps the only one of its kind) or a particular instance. When we say "spend the time", usually we are referring to a specific length of time. (Vacation, for example.) I'm assuming that you are on the computer as a ongoing thing--like it's your job or something you do very often when you have free time.)

 
  • I don't have a good chair to sit on, mine is just wood and some king of straws...

  • I don't have a good chair to sit on. Mine is just wood and some kind of straws... (<--This reads more clearly if you separate it into two sentences. Also, I think the straw you are referring to is what we call "wicker". For some reason, the plural of "straw" that is fed to cows or woven into furniture is just "straw". But if you are referring to the "straw" that one drinks from, then the plural is actually "straws". I have absolutely no idea why that is, but there you have it. : )

 
  • In the last 4 years, I didn't want to buy a correct chair because it's too expensive, and now I feel the same but I can not do another thing...

  • In For the last 4 four years, I didn't have not wanted to buy a correct proper/better chair because it's they are too expensive. and now I still feel the same, but I can not do another thing have no other choice... (<--Much of these changes just make the sentence sound more natural. For example, English speakers would probably say either "proper", "better", or even "another" chair before they would say a "correct" chair. We usually use "correct" to refer to more abstract things, like a decision one makes or the answer to a math problem. "Correct" carries the connotation that if one choice is correct, then the other is probably "incorrect" and therefore completely wrong or bad. The straw chair is not bad, it just isn't the right one for sitting at a desk.)

 
  • So, I resigned myself : I will buy one next week.

  • So, I have resigned myself: I will buy one next week.
