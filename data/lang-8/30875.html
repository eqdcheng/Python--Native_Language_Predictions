  • a horrible experience

  • A Horrible Experience [In English, we capitalize the first letter of each word in the title]

 
  • I have leisure time I often use a PC.(The main is net surfing)

  • When I have leisure time, I often use a the PC. (The main hobby/activity is net surfing)

 
  • Since my external HDD(I don't know right name) memory was full,

  • Since my external HDD (I don't know right name* HHD= Hard Drive Disk, but the easier way to say it is "external drive".]) memory was full, [the word "memory" was removed because when you say an "HDD" is full the reader knows that you mean "memory"]

 
  • I moved the data from it to DVD-R.

  • I moved the data from it to DVD-Rs.

 
  • After moved, I chaked the data of DVD-R.

  • After I moved it, I checked the data of on the DVD-Rs.

 
  • Suddenly display turned blue screen.

  • Suddenly, the display turned showed ["showed" is the better verb in this context] blue screen.

 
  • That's why!!

  • That's Why!?! [Why is a question marker, and sentences with this always end with a: "?"]

 
  • I was panic.

  • I was panicked,

 
  • Because it was the first experience for me.

  • because it was the first time I had this experience for me.

 
  • First of all, I started counting the prime numbers.

  • Firstof all, I started counting the prime numbers

 
  • Next restarted PC.

  • and next I restarted the PC.

 
  • PC was working(running?).

  • The PC was working (running?)*[Both "working" and "running" can be used.],

 
  • After a while,PC turned blue secree again.

  • and after a while, thenPC went blue (screen) again.

 
  • You know,this is a horrible.

  • You know, this is a was horrible.

 
  • Now PC is working properly,but I am afraid of getting blue screen again.

  • Now the PC is working properly, but I am afraid of getting the blue screen again.

 
  • I have been using the PC for five yaers.

  • I have been using the this ["This" is more specific than "the"] PC for five yaers (years);

 
  • That means that it will run out of life in the near future,dosen't it?

  • that means that it will run out of life stop working in the near future,dosen't it?

 
  • I want a new PC,but that no money.

  • I want a new PC, but that no I haven't enough money.

 
  • Please endurance until buy a new PC.

  • Please endurance last [In this context "last" can have the same meaning as "endure" and it sounds more correct.] until I buy a new PC.

That sounds terrible, I hope that you computer lasts long enough for you to buy a new one. I hope that the data you moved was not corrupted or damaged.

Eum, you have good vocabulary but you make small mistakes like omitting articles and subjects [the pronouns]. Also I noticed that you rarely uses conjunctions, [for example "and", "but" etc...] to link related ideas. By not using linking words like "and" your sentences can be grammatically incomplete or awkward.
