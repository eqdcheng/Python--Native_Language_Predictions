Hi! My name is Alex (Sasha in Russian) and I'm from Belarus.
I emigrated to the United states almost five years ago and I live in Brooklyn, NY.
Unfortunately I have no one to speak with and have no abilities to practice my English. This is very sad - to live in English speaking country and have no way to speak and learn language that is all around you. :(
When I first came to the United States I didn't know a single word in English. I remember standing in the McDonalds the first day I came trying to order a French-fries and diet coke - I opened the mouth and... nothing came from it. I was standing and mumbling something. that was horrible.
So, I watched TV and read books - this is the way I learned English.
From now on I will write here some thoughts about everything what bothers me. About emigration and cultural differences, about feelings, about America and my view on it.

Can you please help me to correct my mistakes? Not only grammatical flaws but also the way I speak. Cause the language is not only the words and grammar rules, it is also THE WAY you speak, the way you express your thoughts. Many people speaking foreign language often speak it in a very strange way - the way that natives never speak. Because different languages have different structures and different 'language tools' to express same things. I hope you understand what I'm talking about.

Thank you.

Certainly. Your entry is high level and honestly I think you do just fine, compared to so many "foreigners" that I met when I was in the states. I went along with my British friends who together with me concluded that the country was filled with people that were unable to utter a word in English. But if you wish to learn how to speak as a native american, you can always try getting into a dialect school. Of course this includes a cost, but where there is a will there is a way.
I think it's commendable that you wish to learn the language so much and it should be something rewarded by the society, as this means you can become an invaluable tool in the near future. Most immigrants in the US don't bother learning the language and isolate themselves to areas where they only speak their native tongue. If USA was to poll all power into it's immigrants, getting them to learn the language and offer them a proper education which is funded by the government, then that would be some awesome powerhouse of a country.
Keep in mind, you already do something that so few before you simply didn't even bother thinking about. Kudos to you Sascha!
