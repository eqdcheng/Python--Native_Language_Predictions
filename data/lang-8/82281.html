Because you are talking about hypothetical things, you use the 'subjunctive tense': is --> were; may --> might; will/must --> would.

I never met my (maternal) grandmother either. Hopefully, you have at least been able to meet you other grandmother.

Hi jesspotter!

Instead of "my grandmother is dead", you could say "my grandmother passed away".

For example: "It's too bad that my grandmother passed away".

It sounds much nicer than saying someone "is dead".

Good work! :)
